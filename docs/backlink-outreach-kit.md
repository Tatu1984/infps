## 2. The anchor-text rule (read before submitting anything)

- Let links use your **brand name** ("Infiniti Tech Partners") or a **bare URL** most of the time. Over-optimized keyword anchors ("best devops consultancy") across many external sites look manipulative and can hurt.
- Always point to the **most relevant page**, not just the homepage: a SOC 2 mention → `/insights/soc-2-in-90-days`; an AI directory → your AI service page; a London listing → `/locations/...`.
- One genuinely relevant link from a trusted site > ten from a link farm. **Never buy bulk links.**

## 3. Directory listings (buyer-intent + backlinks)

| Directory | Why | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Clutch.co** | The dominant B2B services directory; buyers shortlist here | Free profile. The ranking lever is **verified client reviews** — line up 2–3 clients to leave one. |
| **GoodFirms** | Second-biggest; similar buyer intent | Free profile + reviews. |
| **The Manifest** | Clutch's sister site; lighter-weight listing | Free. |
| **DesignRush** | Agency directory, decent DA | Free tier exists. |
| **G2** | Only if/when you list a *product* (MDC, TS Edge Nest) | Product reviews, not services. |
| **Local / chamber** | London + NY tech directories, chambers of commerce | Pairs with your Google Business Profiles. |

**Profile copy:** use the Short boilerplate (§1), the category tags, NAP, and link to the homepage **plus** one service page. Keep NAP (name/address/phone) **identical** across every listing and your Google Business Profiles — consistency is a local-SEO ranking signal.

> ⚠️ Directories that ask for payment for a "featured" slot or guaranteed leads are rarely worth it early. The free listing carries the SEO value.

## 6. Expert quotes / digital PR (~30 min/week, editorial links)

Journalists constantly need expert sources; answering well earns a link from a real
publication — among the strongest links you can get.

- **Sign up:** Featured (formerly Terkel), Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer, SourceBottle. Set alerts for: SaaS, DevOps, cloud cost, cybersecurity, SOC 2, AI adoption, CTO.
- **How to answer:** be specific and quotable, 2–4 sentences, lead with a concrete number or a contrarian take (the same hooks that work on LinkedIn). Include your name + "Infiniti Tech Partners" for attribution.
- **Reusable angles you can already speak to:** the real fully-loaded cost of an in-house team (US vs UK); when fractional beats hiring; SOC 2 in 90 days as code; what "production-ready" actually means; why most LLM features fail on cost, not quality.

## 7. Track what lands

Keep a simple sheet: **Target · Type · Date contacted · Status · Live URL · DA**.
Once a month, check Google Search Console → **Links** report to see which domains
now point at you, and confirm the pages you *want* ranking are the ones getting links.
Pair this with the `seo/track.mjs` keyword report to see whether new links move positions.

> **The 80/20:** partner links + 3–4 directory profiles + a steady expert-quote habit
> will out-perform any volume play, and two of the three send buyers, not just crawlers.
